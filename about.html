Where It Started

As a kid growing up in Florida, rocks and minerals always felt like they belonged to another world. Florida is not a place known for dramatic geology, and the idea of finding something like obsidian felt almost mythical. These pieces of Earth carried stories from places that seemed impossibly far away.

The Journey West

Wandering Tortuga Studio grew from a life of exploration and collecting. My camper, which I built myself, became the tool that has allowed me to travel across the American West and search for the stones that speak to me along the way. That collection comes to life here.

Why These Stones

Every stone has something that makes it worth noticing. The colors, patterns, textures, and hidden details tell a story of the immense forces that shaped them. Finding a stone is about more than bringing something home. It is about the landscape it came from, the adventure of finding it, and the challenges overcome to collect them.

Ethically Sourced

The stones offered through Wandering Tortuga Studio are personally gathered whenever possible. Ethical sourcing means knowing where materials come from and respecting the places they come from. I believe in collecting where permitted, avoiding over-collection, and appreciating these landscapes rather than simply taking from them. Personally finding and handling each piece is part of what makes these stones special.

Growing the Craft

This journey continues through learning lapidary, creating cabochons, and exploring simple metalwork. Future pieces may include handmade jewelry and collaborations with other craftspeople who share an appreciation for natural stones and thoughtful design.
